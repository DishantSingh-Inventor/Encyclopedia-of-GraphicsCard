Verdict

The RTX 3090 Ti was Ampere's final stand — a fully unlocked GA102 die with all 10752 CUDA cores enabled and running at the same 1008 GB/s bandwidth as the RTX 4090 thanks to 24GB of GDDR6X on a 384-bit bus. It launched at $1999, which in hindsight is very hard to justify. The Ampere architecture's lower clock speeds (1860 MHz boost vs 2520 MHz on 4090) mean it can't match Ada Lovelace's performance, and the 450W TDP with older DLSS 2 instead of DLSS 3 makes it a tough sell today. Historically important — the last big Ampere — but not a card to buy new.
